Google Workspace Governance

Users, groups, and sharing defaults that keep collaboration fast — and controllable.

The Challenge

As teams grew, Drive shares multiplied: “anyone with the link,” leftover accounts after exits, and no consistent group structure. Collaboration worked — until someone left or a sensitive folder leaked into the wrong inbox.

The Approach

  • Org model — Department groups as the default for Drive and Calendar, not ad-hoc emails.
  • Sharing defaults — Tightened external sharing and required explicit review for broad links.
  • Lifecycle — Joiners get role-based groups on day one; leavers lose access the same day with a checklist.
  • Admin hygiene — Periodic audits of dormant users, external shares, and admin roles.

Results

Onboarding and offboarding became predictable. Sensitive folders stopped travelling via random links. Teams still collaborated quickly — but through owned groups instead of personal sprawl.

Takeaway

Workspace is infrastructure. Treat permissions like ERP access: least privilege, named owners, and a review cadence.
