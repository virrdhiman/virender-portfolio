Month-End Close Acceleration

Structured reconciliations, exception ownership, and a leadership pack that made close faster — and more controlled.

The Challenge

Month-end stretched because reconciliations lived in scattered workbooks. Variances were discovered late. Leadership asked for “the number” while teams were still reconciling. High performers carried tribal knowledge; new joiners slowed the close.

The Approach

  • Close checklist — Sequenced tasks with owners, due times, and dependencies — not a free-for-all email thread.
  • Automated pulls — Standard extracts from ERP and banking sources into a controlled workbook / tracker with refresh timestamps.
  • Exception queues — Material variances routed to named owners with SLA, not buried in tabs.
  • KPI dictionary — One agreed definition for the metrics leadership saw in the close pack.
  • Leadership briefing — A short status pack: green items, open exceptions, and decisions needed — ready before the meeting, not after.

Results

Close became more predictable. Exceptions surfaced earlier. Leadership discussions shifted from “where is the file?” to decisions on material items. The operating rhythm survived personnel changes because ownership was documented.

Takeaway

Accelerating close is not about rushing people — it is about removing ambiguity. When definitions, owners, and exceptions are visible, speed and control improve together.
