About

Operations, systems,
and fewer fire drills

I’ve spent 15+ years in the messy middle of business and technology — ERP, CRM, reporting, and the processes people actually run day to day.

Most of my work is practical: find where time leaks, fix the handoffs, automate the repeatable parts, and give managers dashboards they trust. I’ve led that work at Rhiti Group and earlier roles across consulting and product teams.

Day to day that looks like mapping how work really moves, cleaning master data so systems stop arguing, and building review packs leadership opens before the meeting — not after it. I sit between operations and IT so neither side is left translating alone.

Across ERP platforms (Tally, SAP MM), CRM (Salesforce), Google Workspace, and modern automation/AI tooling, I focus on adoption and outcomes: fewer manual cycles, clearer ownership, and reporting that survives a tough management review.

Lean Six Sigma Green Belt — I care about clean SOPs and measurable outcomes, not slide decks that gather dust. If a process can’t be explained simply, it usually isn’t ready to automate.
